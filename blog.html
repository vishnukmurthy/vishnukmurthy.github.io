Solving for False Expectations (What is Work For)?

Recently, there was an article on the front page of the NY Times titled After Working at Google, I’ll Never Let Myself Love a Job Again, the subtitle goes on to say “I learned the hard way that no publicly traded company is a family”. Immediately, I thought that the entire premise of this article was asinine. Who could ever equate a job with family?

Quickly, I remembered that I was in no position to judge, as I had been guilty of a similar folly not too long ago. Instead of looking for love from a company, I had wanted something from my startup job that was equally ludicrous – to give back to the world by trying to solve a fundamentally political issue: transitioning to clean energy.

At a high level, the problem is that I was looking to fulfill some need in the wrong place. Despite intrinsically knowing that I contain multitudes, I fell into the trap of believing that a predefined role could satisfy me in a more holistic manner than possible.
